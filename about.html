Our Story

About Seen With Care

Fighting to ensure that no matter the situation, everyone receives the love and care that they deserve.

There are over 150 million people in the world living in homelessness. In 2020, we created our first care bags to hand out directly to people living outside. The original bags contained things like granola bars, gloves, and hand sanitizer.

After speaking with recipients, the contents expanded to include small luxuries like postcards and warm socks. We discovered that handing out bags directly fosters a unique sense of giving back, one that cannot be replicated by donating through third-party organizations.

Seeing and interacting with real people, and hearing their stories, has led us on a mission to combat the stigma that surrounds homelessness. Today, we fight tirelessly to ensure that no matter the situation, everyone receives the love and care that they deserve.

"Our mission is twofold: to humanize people living outside through direct aid and conversation, and to destigmatize homelessness among youth and teens."

Our Approach

What Sets Us Apart

We believe in direct connection and genuine care.

1

Direct Delivery

We travel directly to people in need to deliver our bags and get their feedback. This personal connection ensures we understand real needs and can continuously improve our care bags.

2

Stigma Reduction

Besides lessening the plight of homelessness, we concentrate on stigma reduction. We believe changing perceptions is just as important as providing material support.

3

Youth Education

We are particularly focused on educating youth about homelessness. By engaging young people, we're building a more compassionate generation.

4

Dignity First

Our utmost goal is ensuring that people living outside feel dignified and respected. Every interaction is an opportunity to acknowledge someone's humanity.

Our Process

How We Do It

Direct Aid in Action

We help people living outside with direct aid. We create care bags full of everyday supplies including socks, hats, gloves, food, water, personal hygiene kits, and stamped postcards to hand out to the homeless population in our community. We have compiled lists and created tutorials to allow you to do the same in your community.
