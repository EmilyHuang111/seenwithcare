Becoming a Chapter Head

Are you interested in changing the world? Do you view homelessness as an immense societal challenge that has yet to be solved?

A Seen With Care chapter is the perfect opportunity

to apply ideas, strategies, and lessons to combat homelessness in your own city. Homelessness is a worldwide issue and deserves to be acknowledged and addressed in every corner of the globe.

As a Chapter Head, it is your role to make Seen With Care come to life in your community! This can include creating and distributing care bags, hosting events, reaching out to local shelters, starting a club at your school, and overall taking inspiration from Seen With Care to combat and destigmatize homelessness wherever you are.

For Students

Starting a Club at Your School

Get your school community involved and gain leadership experience.

Start a Seen With Care Club

If our mission resonates with you, we encourage you to start a club at your school! This is a great opportunity for you to get your community involved and expand your reach.

How to Get Started:

  1. Find a Sponsor: Reach out to a teacher at your school who might be interested in sponsoring your club.
  2. Make it Official: Once you have a sponsor, reach out to the school club coordinator to officially begin meeting as a club.
  3. Advertise: The beauty of Seen With Care is that there is no commitment necessary‚Äîanyone can come whenever to help create a bag!
  4. Fundraise: Throughout the year, brainstorm fundraising opportunities and take advantage of any funds your school offers for club leaders.
  5. Meet Regularly: We recommend meeting biweekly to build your bags.

If you have any questions at all, we are here for you every step of the way!
